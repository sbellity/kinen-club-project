# The Free Energy Principle & The Markov Blanket
**Author:** Karl Friston (2010)
**Type:** Theory Summary
**Link:** [Nature Reviews Neuroscience (Abstract)](https://www.nature.com/articles/nrn2787) | [PDF (UCL)](https://www.fil.ion.ucl.ac.uk/~karl/The%20free-energy%20principle%20-%20a%20unified%20brain%20theory.pdf)

## Core Thesis
Any self-organizing system that resists entropy (i.e., stays alive) must minimize its **Free Energy**. In practical terms, this means minimizing **Surprise** (or Prediction Error).

## Key Concepts for Kinen

### 1. The Imperative of Prediction
Brains are not "processing inputs." They are **generating predictions**.
- Input is only used to correct errors (Prediction Error).
- **Kinen Mapping**: This matches Titans' "Test-Time Training." The system predicts what comes next; if it's wrong (High Gradient), it learns. If it's right, it ignores it.

### 2. The Markov Blanket
This is the mathematical boundary that separates "Self" from "World."
- **Internal States**: The system's model (Memory).
- **Sensory States**: Inputs from the world.
- **Active States**: Actions taken on the world.
- **Kinen Mapping**:
  - The "Self" is the User + Kinen System.
  - The "Blanket" is the Interface.
  - The goal is to minimize the surprise of the User.

### 3. Active Inference
You can minimize surprise in two ways:
1. **Perception**: Change your model to fit the world (Learning).
2. **Action**: Change the world to fit your model (Behavior).
- **Kinen Mapping**:
  - *Perception*: Updating the Knowledge Graph / Weights.
  - *Action*: Generating code / Performing tasks to align reality with user intent.

### 4. Mathematical Universality
The equation $F = -\ln p(o)$ applies to a single neuron, a brain, and a society.
- **Kinen Mapping**: This provides the mathematical justification for using **Perplexity** as the universal metric across all layers of our fractal architecture.
